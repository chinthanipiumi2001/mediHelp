Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a chronic inflammatory skin condition characterized by patches of dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. It is one of the most common types of eczema and typically begins in early childhood, although it can occur at any age. Eczema is a complex condition influenced by genetic, environmental, and immunological factors.

Symptoms:

  • Itching
  • Dryness
  • Inflammation
  • Blisters
  • Thickened skin

Causes and Triggers:

  • Genetics
  • Immune system dysfunction
  • Environmental factors
  • Skin barrier dysfunction

Treatment:

  • Emollients
  • Topical corticosteroids
  • Topical calcineurin inhibitors
  • Antihistamines
  • Avoidance of triggers

Management and Coping:

  • Skincare routine
  • Avoid scratching
  • Stress management
  • Seeking support
