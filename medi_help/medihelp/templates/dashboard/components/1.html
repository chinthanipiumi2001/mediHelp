Basal Cell Carcinoma

Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) is the most common type of skin cancer, typically developing on sun-exposed areas of the skin, such as the face, head, and neck. It usually grows slowly and rarely spreads to other parts of the body. However, if left untreated, it can cause disfigurement and damage surrounding tissues.

Symptoms:

  • Shiny or pearly bump
  • Reddish patch of skin
  • Scar-like area
  • Sore that bleeds, heals, and then reopens
  • Bump with raised border and central indentation

Causes and Risk Factors:

  • Excessive sun exposure
  • Fair skin
  • History of sunburns
  • Presence of certain genetic conditions
  • Immunosuppression

Treatment:

  • Surgical excision
  • Mohs surgery
  • Cryotherapy
  • Electrodessication and curettage
  • Topical medications (for superficial BCCs)

Prevention:

  • Limiting sun exposure
  • Using sunscreen
  • Wearing protective clothing
  • Avoiding tanning beds
  • Regular skin examinations
