Melanocytic Lesions

Melanocytic lesions are skin growths or moles that originate from melanocytes, the cells responsible for producing melanin, the pigment that gives skin its color. While most melanocytic lesions are benign, some may develop into melanoma, a type of skin cancer. Regular skin examinations and monitoring of moles are important for early detection and treatment.

Types of Melanocytic Lesions:

  • Common mole (nevus)
  • Atypical mole (dysplastic nevus)
  • Congenital nevus
  • Spitz nevus
  • Blue nevus
  • Recurrent nevus

Characteristics:

  • Asymmetry
  • Irregular borders
  • Varied coloration
  • Diameter larger than 6 millimeters
  • Evolution or changes in size, shape, or color over time

Risk Factors for Melanoma:

  • Family history of melanoma
  • Personal history of melanoma or other skin cancers
  • Presence of numerous or atypical moles
  • Excessive sun exposure
  • Fair skin, light eye color, or light hair color

Prevention and Early Detection:

  • Regular self-examinations of the skin
  • Annual skin checks by a dermatologist
  • Use of sunscreen and protective clothing
  • Avoidance of tanning beds and excessive sun exposure
  • Prompt evaluation of any changes in moles or skin lesions
