Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes the rapid buildup of skin cells, leading to scaling, itching, and inflammation of the skin. It often appears as red, scaly patches that may be itchy or painful. Psoriasis can occur on any part of the body, but commonly affects the elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back.

Symptoms:

  • Red patches of skin covered with silvery scales
  • Dry, cracked skin that may bleed
  • Itching, burning, or soreness
  • Thickened, pitted, or ridged nails
  • Swollen and stiff joints (psoriatic arthritis)

Causes and Triggers:

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Immune system dysfunction
  • Environmental factors
  • Stress
  • Infections (e.g., strep throat)

Treatment:

  • Topical treatments (e.g., corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues)
  • Phototherapy (light therapy)
  • Oral medications (e.g., methotrexate, cyclosporine)
  • Biologics (injectable drugs that target the immune system)
  • Lifestyle changes (e.g., stress management, avoiding triggers)

Coping Strategies:

  • Moisturizing the skin regularly
  • Avoiding scratching or picking at plaques
  • Using gentle skincare products
  • Seeking support from healthcare professionals or support groups
  • Managing stress through relaxation techniques or therapy
