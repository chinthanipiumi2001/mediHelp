Melanoma

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops from the pigment-producing cells known as melanocytes. It is the most dangerous form of skin cancer and can spread to other parts of the body if not detected and treated early. Melanoma often appears as a new or existing mole that changes in size, shape, or color.

Symptoms:

  • Asymmetrical shape
  • Irregular borders
  • Varied colors (shades of black, brown, tan, red, white, or blue)
  • Diameter larger than a pencil eraser
  • Evolving appearance

Causes and Risk Factors:

  • Excessive sun exposure
  • History of sunburns
  • Family history of melanoma
  • Fair skin, light eyes, and blond or red hair
  • Presence of many moles or atypical moles

Treatment:

  • Surgical excision
  • Mohs surgery
  • Chemotherapy
  • Immunotherapy
  • Targeted therapy

Prevention:

  • Limiting sun exposure
  • Using sunscreen
  • Wearing protective clothing
  • Avoiding tanning beds
  • Regular skin examinations
